## Significance and Novelty

India bears the highest global burden of multidrug-resistant tuberculosis (MDR-TB), accounting for 27% of worldwide cases. Despite substantial investments in diagnostic infrastructure, the long-term epidemic trajectory remains poorly defined, hampering evidence-based policy planning. This manuscript addresses this critical knowledge gap through rigorous time-series forecasting, providing the first comprehensive, methodologically robust projections of India's MDR-TB burden through 2035.

### Key Contributions:

1. **Methodological Innovation:** We employ Holt-Winters Damped Trend Exponential Smoothing—a sophisticated approach that accounts for the "saturation kinetics" observed in mature public health interventions. This represents a significant advancement over conventional linear projections that fail to capture diminishing returns.

2. **Policy-Actionable Scenarios:** Unlike previous studies that provide single-point estimates, we model three distinct counterfactual futures (Status Quo, Optimistic, Pessimistic), quantifying the "policy gap" between current trajectory and elimination targets.

3. **Economic Quantification:** We provide comprehensive cost-effectiveness analysis demonstrating that aggressive intervention scale-up would avert 217,000 cases over the decade, yielding a 4.4:1 return on investment—a compelling economic argument for immediate policy action.

4. **Uncertainty Quantification:** Through parametric bootstrap analysis (1,000 iterations), we provide rigorous confidence intervals, addressing a major limitation of existing forecasting literature.

5. **Sub-National Granularity:** State-level projections for all 36 states/UTs enable decentralized, precision public health planning.
