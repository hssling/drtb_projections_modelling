# Supplementary Table S1: State-Level MDR-TB Burden Projections (2025-2030)

## Methodology
State-level projections were derived by applying the national Holt-Winters model parameters to state-specific baseline data (2023-2024). States were categorized into three trajectory groups based on current burden, healthcare infrastructure, and treatment success rates.

## Trajectory Group Definitions

### Optimistic Trajectory
- **Characteristics**: Strong primary healthcare, high literacy, good treatment adherence
- **States**: Kerala, Himachal Pradesh
- **Expected outcome**: Likely to achieve 2030 elimination targets even under Status Quo

### Optimistic-Leaning
- **Characteristics**: Above-average infrastructure, improving treatment success rates
- **States**: Gujarat, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Haryana, Goa, Sikkim
- **Expected outcome**: Could achieve elimination with moderate intervention scale-up

### Status Quo
- **Characteristics**: Average national performance, stable but not declining rapidly
- **States**: Majority of states (18 states + 6 UTs)
- **Expected outcome**: Will require aggressive intervention to meet 2030 targets

### Pessimistic-Leaning
- **Characteristics**: High burden, weak infrastructure, low treatment success, high migration
- **States**: Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand
- **Expected outcome**: Risk of burden increase without immediate intensive intervention

---

## Key Insights

1. **Geographic Concentration**: Top 5 states (UP, Maharashtra, Bihar, MP, Rajasthan) account for **62%** of national burden

2. **Heterogeneity**: 10-fold variation in per-capita burden between highest (UP) and lowest (Kerala) states

3. **Urban Hotspots**: Delhi, Mumbai (Maharashtra), Kolkata (West Bengal) show higher burden than state averages

4. **Intervention Priorities**: 
   - **Tier 1 (Immediate)**: UP, Maharashtra, Bihar - account for 50% of national burden
   - **Tier 2 (High)**: MP, Rajasthan, Gujarat, West Bengal
   - **Tier 3 (Moderate)**: Remaining states with >1,000 cases/year

## Assumptions
- State-level damping parameters assumed equal to national average (Ï† = 0.85)
- Private sector contribution estimated at 25-35% depending on state urbanization
- Migration effects not explicitly modeled (limitation)

---

**Note**: These projections should be interpreted as indicative rather than definitive. State-specific models incorporating local epidemiological parameters, healthcare capacity, and intervention coverage would provide more precise estimates.
